Pixel Art? Who me? Yes you!

To make pixel art, you broadly have two choices: (1) use a Pixel Art Editor (2) write code to generate/edit your pixel art. But there are many options too, including combining a Pixel Art editor with writing code or output from generative AI in creative ways. There is no right or wrong way to create pixel art. Let the sky be the limit, and use your imagination!

  • Pixie Engine Editor is a free online tool that offers the very basic features necessary for making pixel art.
  • GIMP is an open source image editor probably best known as the “free alternative” to Photoshop.
  • There are many online lists of other popular tools, such as this one and this one.
  • There are also mobile device apps, like this iOS app and Android app.

Formats and rules, oh dear!

There are two categories of Pixel Art you may submit: 16x16 (meaning 16 pixels wide by 16 pixels high) and 32x32. Each person may submit at most one of each size. We recommend you submit files in PNG or GIF format.

For a given size, we require you submit two versions of the file: one of the actual size, and one scaled up to 160x160.

Animations? Yes, for sure! Animations must be in animated GIF format. There is a limit of 60 frames on the GIF animations.
